edgar-style-policy · a Claude Code writing-style toolkit

Teach Claude to write in your voice.

And keep it there, session after session.

Claude writes well, but not always the way you would. This toolkit turns your preferences into a writing-style policy Claude actually follows: an interview draws out your voice, the words you will not abide, how you format, and how you want claims hedged, then deploys it as layered policy that holds up through long sessions rather than fading after the first few exchanges.

You author it once, and a review step reads each reply to catch the slips. Keep several named styles and switch between them by name; revise any of them as your taste sharpens. All of it happens by talking to Claude, with no terminal to open.

What you can do
1

Author a style

An interview becomes a directive in your voice, reviewed for contradictions and tested before it ships.

2

Make it stick

Four reinforcing layers keep the voice from drifting in long sessions, and a judgment step reviews each reply against your rules.

3

Keep a library

Hold several named styles at once and switch the active one by name, whenever the work calls for a different register.

4

Revise and update

Change a style, or update the toolkit itself, all from inside Claude. Ban a phrase, add an example, and redeploy in a breath.

Getting started

In Claude Code, add the marketplace and install the plugin: /plugin marketplace add garycoding/claude-style-toolkit then /plugin install edgar-style-policy@claude-style-toolkit. In the Mac app's Cowork tab, ask Claude to do it for you; the manual shows how.
